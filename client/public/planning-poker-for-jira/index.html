Estimate Jira Backlog Items With Planning Poker

Planning poker is a consensus-based agile estimation technique: the team discusses a backlog item, everyone votes a card simultaneously, and the hidden reveal exposes disagreement before it turns into a bad estimate. It is the fastest way to turn a noisy sprint planning meeting into a clear set of Jira story points.

If you already track work in Jira, your backlog is your source of truth - the epics, stories, and bugs you need to size for the next sprint. This tool sits next to that backlog and handles the one thing Jira does not do well out of the box: anonymous, simultaneous, distraction-free voting that surfaces real disagreement instead of anchoring on whoever speaks first.

How to Run a Session Next to Jira Today (No Install, No Signup)

You can run a full estimation session right now without touching your Jira admin. Here is the workflow teams use:

  • Open the room. Go to the free planning poker tool and create a session - no account, no email.
  • Invite the team. Share the room link in Slack, Teams, or your video call. Everyone joins in one click, on any device.
  • Bring in the backlog. Copy the Jira story key and title (for example, "PROJ-142 - Add SSO to login") from your board and paste it in as the item being estimated.
  • Vote at the same time. Each person picks a card. Votes stay hidden until everyone is in, so nobody anchors on the loudest voice.
  • Reveal and discuss. Flip the cards, talk through the high and low outliers, and re-vote if needed until you reach consensus.
  • Paste the points back. Take the agreed number and drop it into the Story Points field on that Jira issue. Move to the next key and repeat.

It is a copy-in, estimate, paste-back loop. A little manual, but completely free, instant to start, and it works with Jira Cloud, Jira Data Center, or any other tracker you happen to use. New to the format? Read what is planning poker for a deeper walkthrough of the technique.

What's on the Roadmap: Jira and CSV Import

To be clear and honest: this tool does not yet have a built-in Jira integration. Today you copy story keys and titles in by hand and paste points back by hand - it is deliberately a no-plugin, no-OAuth setup so you can start in seconds without granting access to your Jira data.

Tighter integration is planned. On the public roadmap we have direct Jira import (pull a sprint or JQL query straight into a session) and CSV import (bulk-load a backlog exported from any tool). These are community-prioritized features. If a Jira or CSV import would make your sprint planning faster, vote for it on GitHub - the more votes a feature gets, the sooner it ships.
